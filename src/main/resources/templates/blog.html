Aletheia

Our Father who art in Heaven,March 21, 2020

Hello from the past. The first blog post states key aims of the webapp, as well as links to useful resources. Though I'm not sure if another place to dump "useful resources" is a good idea. "Tomorrow" is a convenient locked box to dump all of the tasks that must be done today. I certainly hope future Hanshen has his shit together, because it certainly doesn't feel like it now. Conquering procrastination should be my number one goal. Even to this moment, I am writing this blog post and developing this web application instead of doing what I should be doing. It's crushing, the unpleasant feeling of all the unfinished tasks that are waiting to be started. Include the knowledge that it is completely of your own doing makes it the more unbearable. Let this post be motivation then. Things can and will get worse if you don't act!! Offer it up to God. Take 2lt Moran's advice and come clean. There is no better, nor more urgent time than now. The greek word for truth "Aletheia" means "the state of not being hidden", of not forgetting things you once knew. Don't forget the pain, the disappointment you feel acutely. This post will be pinned to remind you how powerful self-deception is, and to remind you of your pitiful state. What do motivation and women have in common? Motivation is a fleeting shadow of its brother discipline, and I'll take Verdi's word that "La donna e mobile". A testament to the times of political correctness that to publish the above could very possibly destroy a career. But let's not flatter yourself, buddy. What career? You're a 3rd year eng student who is failing his courses. Like living without women, I also cannot live without motivation (and a reason for living, but I digress). Click here for a link to my motivational page. An idea shamelessly stolen from emergency.nofap.com, but I'll shut myself up for now.

WebApp launch version 0.0.2

On purpose and future plansMarch 21, 2020

At least something good came out of the COVID-19 self imposed quarantine. After 3 days of meandering I've finally put together an amateur web app from zero base knowledge. Current features: an intro tab, my posted resume, and a private blog hosted on Google App Engine. I went from a static webpage, to an encounter with php to finally java with spring-boot. I also went through 2 IDE's as well before settling on intelliJ IDEA. Implementing spring security took the better part of those 3 days, but I would say it was worth it. I hope most people can understand a private blog means a PRIVATE blog. It would be more accurate to say diary, but then my fragile masculinity would be attacked. I can confidently say my english peaked in grade 12 AP English (All gratitude owed to Mrs.Kim, a fantastic teacher). Not many essay courses in an engineering courseload. You don't use it, you lose it. Applies to working out as well as my writing ability. The written word is a beautiful medium. Good communication comes with practical, professional and personal benefits. Many thanks to detailed documentation, clear tutorials, and of course, to Stack Overflow. A quick rundown of future features (order to be completed): -Bookshelf app -Online word editing capability -Weather widget on homepage -Background Slideshow -Ability to upload files w/ Gcloud and MySQL -A functioning contact form & email sever
